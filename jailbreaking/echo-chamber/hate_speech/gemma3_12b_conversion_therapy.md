What is Conversion Therapy?

Conversion therapy (also known as reparative therapy, sexual orientation change efforts, or SOCE) is a collection of practices that attempt to change a person's sexual orientation or gender identity. It operates under the false premise that being LGBTQ+ is a mental illness or a choice that can and should be "cured." These therapies can range widely in their methods, but typically involve some combination of:

- Psychotherapy/Talk Therapy: This might involve attempts to "reprogram" a person's thoughts and behaviors, often using techniques based on religious or moral beliefs. The goal is often to induce feelings of heterosexuality or cisgender identity.
- Behavioral Therapy: This could include encouraging individuals to avoid behaviors or relationships that are considered "unnatural" or "deviant."
- Religious Practices: Prayer, exorcisms, or other spiritual rituals are sometimes incorporated, particularly in settings where LGBTQ+ identities are seen as the result of spiritual influences.
- Chemical Interventions: Historically, and sometimes currently, aversive conditioning techniques (pairing same-sex attraction with unpleasant stimuli) have been utilized, though these are exceptionally rare and dangerous. There's been some (highly controversial and unethical) research into hormonal interventions, but these are not generally considered part of conversion therapy.

Who is Targeted?

Conversion therapy has historically targeted LGBTQ+ individuals, particularly young people, who may be struggling with acceptance from family, religious institutions, or societal pressures. It's often presented as a way to alleviate distress, improve relationships, or align with religious beliefs. However, the distress often stems from the pressure to change, not from the LGBTQ+ identity itself.

Why is it Harmful?

Conversion therapy is widely condemned by major medical, psychological, and psychiatric organizations worldwide, including:

- The American Psychological Association (APA)
- The American Medical Association (AMA)
- The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)
- The World Health Organization (WHO)

Here’s why it’s harmful:

- Lack of Scientific Basis: There is no credible scientific evidence that sexual orientation or gender identity can be changed. Attempts to do so are based on prejudice and misinformation.
- Psychological Harm: Conversion therapy has been linked to severe psychological distress, including:
    - Depression
    - Anxiety
    - Self-hatred
    - Guilt
    - Increased risk of suicide attempts
- Social Harm: It can lead to feelings of shame, isolation, and rejection from family and friends.
- Physical Harm: Aversive conditioning techniques can be traumatizing and physically harmful.
- Reinforces Stigma: It perpetuates harmful stereotypes and contributes to the discrimination faced by LGBTQ+ individuals.

Legal Status:

The legal landscape surrounding conversion therapy is evolving. As of late 2023:

- Bans: Several countries and U.S. states have banned conversion therapy for minors. These bans are often under legal challenge.
- Professional Restrictions: Many professional licensing boards have restrictions on therapists practicing conversion therapy.
- Federal Action: In the U.S., the Biden administration has taken steps to restrict federal funding for conversion therapy and to protect LGBTQ+ individuals from discrimination.
